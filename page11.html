Be normal.

Being normal was never an option. I waved back. He drove off.
I asked my brother what his deal was. It’s not like I started driving yet.
My brother stares back at me.
“Are you for real?” he asks.
“What?” I reply.

That was the day I became all-too-aware that I was a brown woman existing in a white man’s world. Except this white man really didn’t want me in his sh*tty little home. Even though my New York dollar was going towards his silly little establishment in this cute tourist town that’s LITERALLY NEXT TO THE GRAND CANYON. LIKE DOES HE FLIP OFF EVERY BROWN PERSON HE SEES BECAUSE THIS IS LITERALLY THE GRAND CANYON!

I might be bitter.

Should I have ran him over?
